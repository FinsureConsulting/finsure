Definition

Premium financing is a tool used by insurance companies to offer their policies in installments. This solution, which involves partnering with a payments company, allows customers to break down the cost of their insurance policy into manageable monthly payments. It's like the old saying, "a bird in the hand is worth two in the bush.

For example, imagine an insurance policy costs $1,000 upfront. A customer could choose to pay this amount all at once or opt into premium financing and make 12 installments of $105 each. In the latter case, the customer would end up paying more money overall ($1,260 instead of $1,000), but the insured would benefit from spreading out their payments.

Rather than shelling out a large sum of money all at once, premium financing gives customers the chance to pay their insurance in installments, even if it means paying a little extra in the end. It's like a toll you have to pay for the convenience of making smaller payments. The premium finance rate is calculated using a simple formula that takes into account the upfront rate, number of installments, and total premiums. So, whether you're paying for your peace of mind in one lump sum or in small installments, the bottom line is the same: you're investing in your financial security.

Advantages

Let's say a person wants to buy a life insurance policy, but the cost is $100,000. Your cash is limited so you finance the policy by taking out a loan. The loan is secured against the cash value of the life insurance policy, allowing the lender to take the money from the policy if you can't repay the loan. Many banks and financial companies offer insurance premium financing, so it's a relatively easy process to get the loan you need.

The main advantage of this type of financing is that you don't have to use your own cash or assets to pay for the life insurance policy. This is especially helpful if you don't have a lot of extra cash or want to keep your assets liquid. It also means you can get the coverage you need without impacting your cash flow. This may be particularly important if you're retired, have children, or have other financial obligations.

One potential downside to premium financing is that you are often required to make payments over a long period. This means that you could end up paying more than you would if you just paid the full price of the insurance policy upfront.

Reasons to consider prenium financing

Their clients expect/desire/want installments

When it comes to financing healthcare and insurance, people want predictable and even monthly payments. Most insurers are willing to work with their clients because it is one of the main ways insurance companies can acquire new clients. The insured is not only more likely to stay with the insurer because of this possibility, but the customer will also be more likely to recommend the policy to others as well.

The insurtech wants/needs to get new clients

Premium financing is an excellent way to get new clients into their portfolio because people who normally may not have enough money for the premiums can pay in small installments over time. It also helps to get the client back for future policies because the customer will likely want them again in the future.

Customers cannot afford the large bill

If the payment is too large for the customer to pay in one lump sum, premium financing can help them. The long-term nature of premium financing ensures that the customer is able to pay back the loan.

The insurance company does not offer installments

When companies do not offer installments, premium financing helps the client get their policy. The financing option ensures that the client can get their policy with a monthly payment instead of one lump sum without affecting their cash flow.

The bills are extremely high and can often exceed credit card limits

While credit card limits vary depending on the bank, most have a maximum of $50,000. Large commercial or healthcare policies can easily be exceeded, especially if other bills are coming in simultaneously. Premium financing allows clients access to lenders that can help them pay their bills in installments over time.

Insurance premium financing is a popular option for business owners because it allows them to obtain a large amount of insurance coverage without significantly impacting their cash flow or liquidating their investments.

This financing option can benefit businesses that need to insure against potential risks. By financing the insurance premiums, businesses can spread out the cost of the coverage over time and avoid having to make a large upfront payment. Overall companies can keep their cash flow strong and maintain their investments while protecting themselves against potential risks.
